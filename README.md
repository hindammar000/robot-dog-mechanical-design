# Cognitive Quadruped Robot – Mechanical Design

## Overview
This project presents the preliminary mechanical design of a cognitive quadruped robot inspired by the Stanford Pupper platform. 
The robot is designed to provide a lightweight, stable, and modular structure while supporting future integration of Artificial
Intelligence and Computer Vision.

This project was developed as part of a Robotics course to demonstrate the fundamentals of mechanical robot design.

---

## Objectives

- Design a stable quadruped robot chassis
- Select suitable construction materials
- Define the robot's Degrees of Freedom 
- Select appropriate actuators.
- Perform preliminary torque calculations
- Analyze stability and center of gravity
- Propose a walking gait
- Identify expected mechanical challenges

---

## Mechanical Specifications

- **Robot Type:** Quadruped Robot
- **Body Shape:** Rectangular chassis with rounded corners
- **Material:** PLA+
- **Dimensions:** 350 × 180 × 90 mm
- **Number of Legs:** 4
- **Degrees of Freedom:** 12 DoF (3 joints per leg)
- **Actuators:** Servo Motors
- **Walking Gait:** Trotting Gait
